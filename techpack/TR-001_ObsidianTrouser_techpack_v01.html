04

Construction & finishing

  • Waistband. Curtained inside, 4 cm finished; 5 self-fabric belt loops. Close with concealed hook & bar + interior button. Loops must carry the leather belt without gaping.
  • Fly. Left-over-right, YKK metal zip; J-topstitch 6 mm with a bartack at the base. Fly length 16 cm (base S).
  • Front pockets. Slant, understitched bag, bartack both ends. Pocketing in Bone contrast.
  • Centre-back seam. Double-stitched through the curve; leave 1.5 cm SA for let-out — do NOT trim.
  • Back shaping. Two darts per side, pressed toward centre back. Clean seat — no back pockets (design intent).
  • Crease. Pressed centred through the leg with a press cloth — the wool must not shine.
  • Side seams. Pressed open, edges overlocked separately.
  • Hem. Blind-stitched, 5 cm allowance, invisible on the face; even break. Wide leg hangs straight from the hip.
05

Labelling & packing

  • Brand label woven, centre-back inside the waistband. Care/content + fibre + CoO on the side-seam label; size tab beneath.
  • Content/care per destination market; symbols per ISO 3758. Country of origin per your facility.
  • Pack: hung on a clipped hanger, or folded to a set board; polybag food-grade if used.

To quote — what we need from you

First development sheet. Please make one fit proto, size S, Obsidian, and quote against a small house-inventory run.

  • Sample cost + lead time for the size-S proto.
  • Unit cost at MOQ 10 / 30 / 50, plus a small-batch cost so we can house a first run.
  • Fabric: can you source the matte stretch suiting + Bone pocketing, or should we supply? Send suiting options.
  • Grading: confirm the graded nest and the POM tolerances above; flag anything that risks the wide-leg drape.
  • Value engineering: call out any change that protects the look while improving cost or lead time.
