# Te Amo — Women's Sizing Playbook

*A production/merchandising reference, not theme code. Based on a fact-checked deep-research pass.
Verified claims are marked **[verified]**; industry-convention values that still need validation
against real fit samples are marked **[convention — validate]**.*

## TL;DR recommendation

**Don't replace S/M/L — augment it.** The problem isn't the alpha label; it's that alpha *alone*
tells a woman nothing about whether a garment fits her body. Fix it with three layers:

1. **Label every size as `AU 8 / S`** — lead with the AU/NZ number, keep the alpha as a familiar tag.
2. **Publish a body-measurement chart** (bust / waist / hip + category-specific points) as **ranges,
   in both cm and inches**, on every product page.
3. **Offer made-to-measure** alongside standard sizes — your made-to-order model makes this a
   genuine advantage, and it's what removes "what size am I?" anxiety entirely.

This mirrors the Australian peer brand **Aje** (numeric-first, alpha secondary, bust/waist/hip
ranges in cm + in) **[verified]** and is consistent with the US body-measurement standard
**ASTM D5585-21**, which publishes *body* measurements (designers add ease) in independent cm and
inch tables, graded non-uniformly across body points **[verified]**.

## 1. The labelling scheme

| Where | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Variant option (Shopify) | `AU {n} / {alpha}` | `AU 8 / S` |
| Size guide heading | AU/NZ numeric, alpha in parens | `Size 8 (S)` |
| Conversions (in guide) | AU · UK · US · EU | `AU 8 · UK 8 · US 4 · EU 36` |

Lead with **AU/NZ numeric** (home market, ACCC jurisdiction), because it carries body-size meaning
the alpha label lacks. Keep the alpha so international shoppers aren't lost, and expose the full
conversion + measurements one tap away.

never single numbers.**

## 4. "How to measure yourself" (put this in the size guide) **[verified]**

- **Bust** — around the fullest part of the bust.
- **Underbust / band** — snug, directly under the bust (for bras / supportive swim).
- **Waist** — around the smallest part of the waist.
- **Hip** — around the fullest part of the hip.
- Keep the tape **parallel to the floor**, snug not tight, measure over light clothing.
- **Between two sizes?** See the per-collection note below — the answer differs by category.

## 5. Per-collection fit logic (they are genuinely different)

**Gym / Activewear** — compression, 4-way stretch. Cut deliberately tighter than fashion.
- Add **inseam** (leggings/shorts) and **underbust + full bust** (sports bras).
- Between sizes: **size up for comfort, size down for a snugger/compressive feel** **[verified, JOLYN]**.
- Sports bras: publish **band + cup** for supportive styles; S/M/L is acceptable only for soft bralettes.

**Swimwear** — one-pieces live or die on **torso length**.
- For one-pieces the **torso loop (shoulder-through-crotch girth) is the *lead* measurement**, above
  bust/waist/hip **[verified, JOLYN]** — publish it.
- Bikini bottoms: hip + rise/coverage. Bikini tops: band + cup preferred over alpha *(open — your call)*.
- Same "runs tight → size up for comfort" guidance as activewear.

**Dress / Eveningwear / Tailoring** — hangs on the **bust–waist–hip triad**; the strictest fit.
- Publish all three plus **hollow-to-hem** for gowns.
- Eveningwear convention: **cut to the largest of the three measurements, then tailor in** *(open —
  conventional but not evidenced; confirm with your atelier)*. This is exactly where made-to-measure wins.

## 6. Made-to-order / made-to-measure

Your model (techpack → sample → house inventory) lets you offer **made-to-measure on top of standard
sizes** — each garment adjusted from a base block to the customer's measurements **[verified concept]**.

- Present on the PDP as a choice: **"Standard size"** vs **"Made to my measurements."**
- Collect at order time: height, bust, underbust, waist, high hip, low hip; + **inseam** (trousers/leggings),
  + **torso loop** (swim one-pieces), + hollow-to-hem (gowns).
- Apply **ease/tolerance per fabric** (compression stretch vs bias silk vs structured tailoring) —
  *this is an atelier decision, not researchable; set it from your sample fittings.*
- **Do NOT market MTM as "eliminates overstock" or lean on 3-D body-scan claims** — both were
  *refuted* in verification. Sell it as fit and fewer returns.

## 7. Launch size range

- Because you're **made-to-order, the usual "buy depth per size" risk barely applies** — extending the
  range costs you a pattern grade, not inventory. That's a real advantage; use it.
- Suggested standard ladder: **AU 4–16** at launch, with **made-to-measure covering everything beyond**.
- If you offer extended sizing, resource it like a core category (imagery, fit validation), not a token
  add-on **[verified]** — but don't use "validate core fit first" as a reason to *delay* inclusion
  indefinitely (a real reputational counterweight raised in the research).

## 8. Shopify / theme implementation

- **Variant naming:** rename the `Size` option values from `XS/S/M/L` to `AU 6 / XS … AU 16 / XXL`
  (update the demo product data in `templates/page.*.json` + `collection.*.json`, and the PDP/card
  Liquid). Keep it one option so the cart/checkout stays clean.
- **Size guide:** a **per-collection modal** on the PDP with a **cm/inch toggle** — gym, swim and dress
  each get their own chart (they publish different points). A size-chart app (e.g. MP/AVADA) does this,
  **but** that app may have been pulled from the App Store — and this theme is deliberately app-free and
  hand-authored, so **hand-building the modal fits better**: a `snippets/size-guide.liquid` + a small
  block in `base.css` + a toggle in `store.js`, wired to the product's collection.
- Reuse the existing pattern: sizes already live as data in the templates (`"sizes": "XS, S, M, L"`),
  so this is a data + one-snippet change, not a rebuild.
